# Enterprise Expansion

[[Grain]]'s push to move from individual/SMB notetaking to company-wide meeting system of record. The Q2 2026 strategic theme. Encompasses [[Grain Sharing Model]] fixes, [[PQL Pipeline]], team setup simplification, and pricing/packaging changes.

## Update 2026-04-23 from [[2026-04-23_pre-board-conversation-april-2026-1]]

### MCP as the enterprise growth driver

[[Mike Adams]] argued the [[Grain MCP]] connector signal should be the centerpiece of the Q2 board deck, not a footnote:

> "We've never seen signal like the MCP signal. And it feels like the deck is just kind of casually mentioned opposed to building a strategy around it... the connector is what drives the enterprise sale."

On Q2 focus: "This quarter, it's all about land and expand. Team level adoption."

### Expansion speed as the bottleneck

The core business problem: conversions happen but they are tiny (1-2 seat average). Expansion from 1 seat to 20 is too slow. Mike's framing:

> "If we doubled or tripled or quadrupled the rate at which we go from one seat to 20 seats, we would have a fundamentally different business."

### Q3 vision: paid acquisition and enterprise sales

Mike outlined Q3 as the quarter for paid acquisition, targeted ads, and enterprise sales with $25K+ ACVs. Including battle cards against competitors and potentially hiring a dedicated salesperson.

### Teams UX as a blocker

Teams feature is architecturally sound but the UX is "absolutely horrific." Mike: "The number one thing I need to know when I click on a team is who's in the team. And you can't see it."

Agent-based setup proposed: an AI setup bot that can infer team structure from meeting patterns and configure Grain accordingly.

## Update 2026-05-05 from [[2026-05-05_q2-2026-board-follow-up]]

### Q2 objectives finalized

Four objectives for Q2:
1. Develop agentic transformation point of view with meetings at center
2. Expand MCP value and distribution (including product marketing)
3. Build PQL pipeline and figure out business model through sales conversations
4. Address structural product barriers to company-wide expansion

### Product marketing gap

Mike pushed hard on product marketing being under-invested:

> "If we don't have a conversation this quarter, we may miss it. And if we're in the conversation, we don't [need to worry]."

Goal: 10K MCP connector installs. Requires going beyond LinkedIn posts to Reddit ads, re-engagement campaigns to 300K+ email subscribers, and Anthropic co-marketing.

### Enterprise vs. company-level language

[[Ryan Johnson]] pushed back on the word "enterprise" as too loaded. Team aligned on "company-level" or "organizational-level" to describe the scope without implying Fortune 500 sales motions.

## Update 2026-05-07 from [[2026-05-07_board-follow-up-2]]

### Meeting layer framing

Mike proposed "meeting layer" as alternative to "system of record" after discovering that Otter's Sam started using "system of record" language. The concept: Grain as infrastructure, not application. A meeting layer that plugs into the broader agentic stack.

### Knowledge graph as enrichment

Ryan articulated the hope: "Our efforts can be to enrich the data that people have in their meetings with a condensed form of the history" because LLMs are still not good enough at fully using large corpuses of raw transcripts.

Jeff on the strategic bet: start with meetings as the knowledge base, prove the value, and expand when users demand "can you get more stuff in here."
