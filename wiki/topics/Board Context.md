## Update 2026-04-22 from [[2026-04-22_q1-2026-board-meeting]]

Q1 2026 board meeting. 121 minutes. Detailed discussion on Q1 results and Q2 planning. See [[Grain Strategy]] for strategic direction updates.

## Update 2026-04-23 from pre-board conversations

Four pre-board conversations between Jeff and Mike walking around outside. Major themes:

### CEO role discussion
Mike and Jeff had a candid conversation about leadership. Mike expressed interest in potentially returning as CEO but wanted to give Jeff the quarter to prove himself. Jeff was open to it: "I want to do what maximizes the likelihood for me to get a win."

Mike's framework: through Q2, Jeff is CEO. Revisit in Q3 based on results. Mike would focus on being an effective chairman, helping Jeff fill gaps in strategy articulation and storytelling.

### Barrels and ammunition
Jeff introduced the Keith Rabois "barrels and ammunition" framework. His assessment: "I feel like I'm the only barrel." Neither Jake Bagley, Jake Adams, Ben, nor Ryan are barrels in the current setup. Mike agreed he could be a barrel but needs to be "two feet in."

### Personnel assessments
- [[Ryan Johnson]]: "High, high, high leverage ammunition" but not a barrel. Doing his best work recently.
- [[Ben DeMordaunt]]: "Mad at everything" — competent but not great at any one thing. Opportunity cost discussion.
- [[Chris Naismith]]: "Super mid." Needs deep validation, which leads to posturing. Under evaluation.
- Andrew Clarkson: Likely to be let go. On a PIP-equivalent process.

### MCP investment urgency
Mike pushed for an all-company sprint on MCP despite the connector not yet being approved:

> "Hold all the other presses. We're gonna maximize this trend right now for a week... That's actually a pretty decent idea."

Jeff preferred waiting for the connector approval. Mike argued: "Every day that we're not maximizing that is missed opportunity because it's happening on its own and we're not even in the connector."
